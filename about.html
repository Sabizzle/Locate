About loCATE!

A community-driven platform dedicated to reuniting families and finding missing persons across Jamaica

Our Mission

loCATE! was created with a single purpose: to help bring missing persons home safely. We believe that by harnessing the power of community and technology, we can make a real difference in the lives of families experiencing the trauma of a missing loved one.

Every missing person report represents a family in crisis, a community concerned, and a life that matters. Our platform provides the tools, visibility, and coordination needed to maximize the chances of a safe return.

How It Works

1. Report a Missing Person

Family members or concerned parties can submit a detailed missing person report including photos, physical description, last known location, and other relevant information. Reports are reviewed by our moderation team to ensure accuracy and prevent abuse.

2. Community Awareness

Once verified, the report is made visible to the public and alerts are sent to registered users in the relevant parish and surrounding areas. The more people who know, the better the chances of finding the missing person.

3. Gather Information

Community members can submit tips anonymously or with contact information. All tips are reviewed and shared with the reporter and relevant authorities while maintaining confidentiality.

4. Coordinate Search Efforts

When there's enough community interest, search parties can be organized to conduct physical searches of the area. Volunteers receive notifications and can coordinate their efforts through the platform.

5. Resolution & Support

When a person is found, the case is updated and all volunteers are notified. Even closed cases remain in our database as a resource for future reference.

Reporting Guidelines

‚ö†Ô∏è Important Guidelines to Prevent False Reports

  • Only report someone as missing if they cannot be contacted through normal means
  • Check with friends and family members before filing a report
  • Adults have the right to leave - only report if you have genuine concern for their safety
  • Provide accurate and truthful information only
  • File a police report (119) for urgent situations
  • Do not submit false or misleading reports - this is a serious offense

What Makes a Good Report?

  1. Recent, clear photo: The most important element for identification
  2. Specific details: Height, weight, distinguishing features, clothing
  3. Accurate location: Exact address or landmark of last known location
  4. Timeline: Specific date and time last seen
  5. Context: Circumstances of disappearance, if known
  6. Contact information: How people can reach you with tips

Safety Considerations

  • Never confront someone if you think you've spotted a missing person
  • Call the reporter or police with your information
  • Do not put yourself in danger during search efforts
  • Respect private property during searches
  • Work with local authorities and coordinate your efforts
